By Alicia Malay

I've spent over a decade in luxury residential real estate — telling the stories of homes and the people who inhabit them. What I kept watching, in every listing, every transition, every estate sale, was the one element no one was addressing. The energy a space holds long after the people inside it have gone.

MOKSHA is the practice I built around that. Load-Bearing is where I write about it. Property by property. What I find in a space, what surprises me, and what I'm learning about why some homes move and others don't.

What you'll find here

Essays on energetic clearing, listing preparation, the psychology of space, and what it actually takes to prepare a home for what comes next.

Written for agents, homeowners, and anyone who has ever walked into a room and felt something they couldn't name.

→ Start here: Why Some Homes Don't Move

→ Work with me: mokshaspace.co

The name MOKSHA is drawn from Sanskrit, from Alicia's Indian heritage. It carries the meaning of liberation — the release of what no longer serves. That is exactly what this practice offers. Not to the people alone. To the spaces they inhabit.
